## Module B1 — Proteasome (C-terminal proxy)

This module is the **first link** in department B.
It models a key and often misunderstood step:
the **generation of the peptide by the proteasome**.

Without correct peptide generation,
no HLA presentation is possible.

---

### Biological context (intuition without background required)

In the cell, proteins are cleaved by a large enzymatic machinery:
the **proteasome**.

This cleavage:
- is **not random**,
- preferentially produces certain fragments,
- strongly depends on the amino acid **located just after the cleavage site**.

For a peptide presented by MHC-I:
- the **proteasomal cleavage defines its C-terminus**,
- the C-terminus of the peptide is therefore an **indirect signal** of its generation.

👉 In other words:
> *If a peptide ends with an amino acid rarely produced by the proteasome,
> it is less likely to actually exist in the cell.*

---

### Biological hypothesis (explicitly stated)

Experimental analyses show that:
- certain amino acids are **over-represented** at the C-terminal position
 of HLA-I peptides,
- these preferences partly reflect the cleavage biases of the proteasome.

This module therefore implements the following hypothesis:

> *"Peptides whose C-terminus corresponds to a residue
> frequently observed at the C-terminal position of HLA-I ligands
> are more likely to be generated by the proteasome."*

---

### What this module models (and what it does not)

#### Modeled (proxy)
- general preferences of the proteasome,
- via the C-terminal amino acid of the peptide.

#### Not modeled
- the complete upstream sequence,
- cleavage kinetics,
- the different types of proteasomes (constitutive, immunoproteasome),
- contextual cellular effects.

👉 This is deliberately a **highly simplified proxy**.

---

### Expected work (detailed logic)

#### 1. Examine the last amino acid

- Extract the last character of `candidate.peptide_mut`.
- Verify that it is a valid amino acid.

---

#### 2. Favor residues frequently observed at the HLA C-terminus

Accepted approaches:

- define a list of favored residues (e.g., hydrophobic),
- use a simple preference table,
- classify AAs as "favored / neutral / disfavored".

Examples of commonly used intuition:
- hydrophobic residues at the C-terminus → often favored,
- highly charged residues → often disfavored.

⚠️ It is **not necessary** to be exhaustive or exact.
The choice must be **consistent and documented**.

---

#### 3. Implement a documented heuristic

- The score must be calculated **solely** from the C-terminus.
- The logic must be:
 - simple,
 - deterministic,
 - explicitly explained in the report.

Examples of valid heuristics:
- fixed score table per AA,
- penalty if AA is not in the favored set,
- score proportional to a qualitative preference.

---

### Inputs used

- `candidate.peptide_mut`

This module must **not** use:
- the WT peptide,
- the HLA,
- other modules.

---

### Expected output (interpretation)

- **Score close to 0 or slightly positive**
 → C-terminus compatible with plausible proteasomal generation.

- **Negative score**
 → unfavorable C-terminus, peptide less likely to be generated.

The link between C-terminus and score must be clearly explained.

---

### Edge cases to handle

- Empty peptide
- Length < 1
- Non-standard amino acids

In these cases:
- return an explicit penalty or a documented neutral score,
- never crash the pipeline.

---

### Common pitfalls (to absolutely avoid)

- ❌ Using the entire sequence (that is not the purpose)
- ❌ Trying to predict the exact cleavage site
- ❌ Implementing an unjustified binary filter
- ❌ Using rules that are too complex without supporting data

---

### Limitations to state explicitly in the report

- The C-terminus alone is not sufficient to predict actual generation.
- The role of the proteasome is coarsely approximated.
- The immunoproteasome is not distinguished.

---

### What this module does NOT claim to do

- It does not simulate protein degradation.
- It does not guarantee that a peptide will be produced.
- It does not replace a proteasome model.

It provides a **plausibility signal**,
intended to be combined with TAP, ERAP, and HLA.

---

### Critical question you must be able to answer

> *"Why is the C-terminus of this peptide
> favorable or unfavorable in your model,
> and how is this choice biologically reasonable?"*

If you can clearly answer this question,
then your module correctly captures the intended biological intuition.
