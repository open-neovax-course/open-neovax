## Module B2 — TAP (Transporter Associated with Antigen Processing, proxy)

This module models the **second major biological barrier** in the pathway
of MHC-I peptides: **transport to the endoplasmic reticulum (ER)**.

Even if a peptide is correctly generated by the proteasome,
it must still be **actively transported** to be loaded onto the HLA.

---

### Biological context (intuition without background required)

After their generation in the cytosol, peptides do not freely float
to the MHC.

They must be transported across the endoplasmic reticulum membrane
by a specialized protein complex: **TAP**.

This transport:
- is **selective**,
- consumes energy,
- depends on simple properties of the peptide.

👉 TAP therefore acts as an **additional filter**.

---

### Biological hypothesis (explicitly stated)

Experimental studies have shown that TAP:
- preferentially transports peptides
 whose **C-terminus is hydrophobic**,
- is sensitive to the **overall composition** of the peptide
 (charge, hydrophobicity, size).

This module therefore implements the following hypothesis:

> *"Peptides presenting a C-terminus and overall composition
> compatible with TAP preferences
> are more likely to be transported to the ER
> and therefore presented by MHC-I."*

---

### What this module models (and what it does not)

#### Modeled (proxy)
- general preferences of TAP,
- via:
 - the C-terminus,
 - the overall composition of the peptide.

#### Not modeled
- the actual kinetics of transport,
- ATP-dependent effects,
- fine differences between similar peptides,
- competition between peptides.

👉 This is a **qualitative proxy**, not a mechanistic model.

---

### Expected work (detailed logic)

#### 1. Use the C-terminus

- Extract the last amino acid of `candidate.peptide_mut`.
- Favor hydrophobic or neutral residues.
- Penalize highly charged or disfavored residues.

⚠️ The C-terminus is a **key signal for TAP**,
but it should not be the sole criterion.

---

#### 2. Use the overall peptide composition

You can integrate, for example:
- the proportion of hydrophobic amino acids,
- the overall net charge (simple proxy),
- the excessive presence of charged residues.

The goal is to approximate whether the peptide is:
- easily "transportable",
- or disfavored by TAP.

---

#### 3. Implement a simple and justified score

The TAP score must:
- combine **a few simple criteria**,
- be continuous and interpretable,
- avoid overly complex rules.

Examples of accepted approaches:
- score = (C-terminal bonus) + (composition bonus),
- progressive penalty if charge/hydrophobicity is extreme,
- qualitative table (favored / neutral / penalized).

The choice must be **documented** and **biologically defended**.

---

### Inputs used

- `candidate.peptide_mut`

This module must **not** use:
- the WT peptide,
- the HLA,
- scores from other modules.

---

### Expected output (interpretation)

- **Score close to 0 or slightly positive**
 → peptide compatible with plausible TAP transport.

- **Negative score**
 → peptide disfavored by TAP, less likely to reach the ER.

The score must be interpretable in simple biological terms.

---

### Edge cases to handle

- Empty peptide
- Length outside the 8–11 aa range
- Non-standard amino acids

In these cases:
- return an explicit penalty or a documented neutral score,
- never raise an uncontrolled exception.

---

### Common pitfalls (to absolutely avoid)

- ❌ Reproducing exactly the score from module B1
- ❌ Using only the C-terminus without overall composition
- ❌ Implementing an overly complex or opaque model
- ❌ Introducing external dependencies

---

### Limitations to state explicitly in the report

- TAP does not recognize only the C-terminus.
- Exact preferences depend on the experimental context.
- The score is a **coarse proxy**, not a quantitative prediction.

---

### What this module does NOT claim to do

- It does not simulate the actual transport of the peptide.
- It does not guarantee that a peptide will reach the ER.
- It does not replace dedicated TAP models.

It provides a **complementary signal** to the proteasome,
reinforcing or attenuating the plausibility of presentation.

---

### Critical question you must be able to answer

> *"Why is this peptide favored or penalized
> by your TAP score,
> and how is this decision biologically reasonable
> despite the simplicity of the model?"*

If you can clearly answer this question,
then your module correctly fulfills its role.
